Frequently Asked Questions

How does it know how to group files?

FileSeek uses Google's advanced AI to "read" and understand the content of your files—not just the text, but the context and subject matter. It acts like a brilliant librarian, identifying common themes and relationships between documents, images, and other files to propose a logical folder structure.

How does it find a file from a description?

This is called **semantic search**. Instead of matching exact keywords in a filename, FileSeek converts your description (e.g., "the financial report from last quarter") and the content of your files into mathematical representations of their meaning. It then finds the files whose meaning is closest to your query, allowing you to search by thought, not just by keyword.

What kind of folders can I use?

You can select any folder on your Mac. When you want to organize a folder or search within it, FileSeek will prompt you with the standard macOS file picker to grant it temporary, secure access. It only ever accesses the folders you explicitly choose for each operation.

Why do I have to use a personal API key?

This is a core design choice for two crucial reasons: **cost and privacy.**

1. No Subscription Fee: By using your own API key, FileSeek leverages the generous free tier provided by Google AI Studio. For the vast majority of users, this is more than enough for daily use at no cost. If you are a power user and hit the free rate limit (a certain number of requests per minute), you simply need to wait a moment for it to reset. This model means you don't pay us a recurring fee. If you need unlimited power, you can upgrade your *own* Google account to a paid plan and pay Google directly only for what you use.

2. Unparalleled Privacy and Security: Your personal API key creates a secure, direct tunnel from your Mac to your own Google AI account. Your files are **never** sent to our servers or any other centralized service. This is the most private way to use a cloud AI, ensuring only you and Google ever process your data, under your own account's privacy settings.
